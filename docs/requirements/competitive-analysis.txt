# Competitive Analysis
# Source: initial call recording (docs/initialcall.md)

--------------------------------------------------
1) Duolingo — Video Call Feature (Lily)
--------------------------------------------------
Overview:
- Premium-tier feature ("Duolingo Max") at ~$30/month.
- A simulated video call with an AI character named Lily.
- Lily speaks only in the target language (does not respond in English).
- Open-ended conversation — no fixed topic, Lily adapts to learner.

How it works (observed behavior):
- Push-style input: user taps button to start recording, taps again to stop.
- Lily responds with audio in the target language.
- At end of session, user rates difficulty (too easy / too hard); Lily adjusts next time.
- Lily will redirect conversation away from certain topics (guardrails exist).
- Powered by GPT-4 (per 2023 Duolingo blog post; may have updated since).
- NOT using a native voice-to-voice model — confirmed to be STT -> LLM -> TTS.

Mandarin Chinese availability:
- NOT officially released for Mandarin on Android as of call date.
- A workaround exists: on the Practice page, the video call button flickers
  briefly on load — users can tap it before it disappears to initiate a session.
- Cantonese: shows a "not supported yet" message when accessed via workaround.
- May be officially available on iOS (unconfirmed by team).

Weaknesses identified:
1. No vocabulary restriction to learner's level
   - Lily regularly uses words that are not taught in the Duolingo course.
   - This is a significant flaw for learners studying for specific exams (e.g., HSK).
   - Duolingo guards the topic/content of conversation but not vocabulary level.

2. No pronunciation feedback
   - Lily cannot detect mispronunciation.
   - At most, Lily will say "I don't understand" — no specific guidance on what
     was wrong or how to fix it.

3. English-only coaching impossible
   - Lily responds only in the target language.
   - Beginners cannot get English explanations of errors.

4. Model may be undersized
   - Observed failure: Lily could not recognize "Texas Rangers" or "Houston Astros"
     in Mandarin, even after user explicitly mentioned American baseball teams
     in context.
   - Hypothesis: model is either too small or has weak Mandarin sports knowledge.
   - Using STT -> LLM -> TTS likely removes nuance, and a smaller model may
     have less Mandarin coverage for niche topics.

5. AI-generated lesson quality degradation
   - Duolingo's shift toward heavy LLM use has introduced quality issues across
     the platform (not just video call) — listening exercises filled with words
     never taught in the course.

Our Differentiation vs Duolingo:
- HSK vocabulary restriction (primary differentiator — Duolingo has nothing like this).
- Bilingual coaching (English explanations available for errors).
- Voice-to-voice model (preserves pronunciation for actual assessment).
- Pronunciation feedback (goal — not possible with Duolingo's architecture).
- Actually works for Mandarin without a workaround.

--------------------------------------------------
2) Pingo AI
--------------------------------------------------
Overview:
- Observed via social media (Instagram Reels), not directly tested by team.
- Appears to be a voice-based AI language learning app.
- Has attracted attention with emotional user reaction videos (learners frustrated
  with difficult pronunciation, e.g., English learners crying over pronunciation,
  Mandarin learners struggling with tones).

What is known:
- Operates in a similar space: AI voice tutor for language learning.
- Has some kind of voice interaction feature.
- Unknown: vocabulary restriction capability, pronunciation detection accuracy,
  pricing, supported languages, model architecture.

Status: Not evaluated. Worth a direct trial to understand feature parity.

Action item: One team member should test Pingo AI and report findings.

--------------------------------------------------
3) Generic LLM Chat (ChatGPT, etc.)
--------------------------------------------------
Used informally for language practice by many learners.

Weaknesses for language learning:
- No vocabulary restriction: immediately uses advanced vocabulary,
  idiomatic expressions, and complex sentence structures.
- Not designed for teaching: responds to communicate, not to instruct.
- Complexity escalates quickly — not appropriate for HSK1-3 learners.
- No pronunciation assessment (text-only in most interactions).
- No structured correction loop or retry mechanism.

Conclusion: The gap between vanilla LLM chat and a purpose-built tutor
is large. This is the core opportunity for our product.

--------------------------------------------------
5) Market Observation
--------------------------------------------------
- Even the market leader (Duolingo) is not done with AI-powered Mandarin voice practice.
- The space is not saturated — significant room for a more focused, better-quality product.
- The core insight: existing tools guard conversation topic but not vocabulary level.
  Vocabulary-level restriction is an unexplored and high-value differentiator.
- Voice-to-voice model architecture is a technical moat if pronunciation detection
  can be delivered reliably.
